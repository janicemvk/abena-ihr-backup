BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Field of the Invention
This invention relates generally to blockchain technology, cryptocurrency systems, gamification for healthcare, and supply chain verification, and more specifically to systems and methods for incentivizing healthy behaviors through blockchain-based cryptocurrency rewards, and for verifying authenticity and quality of dietary supplements through immutable blockchain-tracked supply chains.
Description of Related Art
Healthcare Adherence Crisis:
Patient non-adherence to treatment regimens is a critical problem in healthcare:

Medication adherence rates: 50% or lower for chronic diseases
Annual cost of non-adherence: $300 billion in the U.S. alone
Preventable hospitalizations: 125,000 deaths per year attributable to medication non-adherence
Lifestyle intervention compliance: <30% for diet and exercise recommendations

Traditional approaches to improving adherence have limited effectiveness:

Reminder systems: Phone calls, text messages, pill organizers (compliance improvement: 5-15%)
Patient education: Knowledge alone doesn't change behavior (compliance improvement: 10-20%)
Physician counseling: Time-intensive, not scalable (compliance improvement: 15-25%)

Gamification in Healthcare:
Gamification—applying game design elements to non-game contexts—has shown promise for behavior change:

Fitness apps with badges and leaderboards increase exercise by 20-40%
Smoking cessation apps with achievement systems improve quit rates by 15-30%
Diabetes management apps with point systems improve glucose control

However, existing gamification systems have critical limitations:

No Tangible Value: Digital badges and points have no real-world utility
Centralized Control: Companies can arbitrarily change reward systems
No Portability: Achievements locked to single platform, cannot transfer
Trust Issues: Users cannot verify if rewards are fairly distributed
No Financial Incentive: Points cannot be redeemed for valuable goods/services

Blockchain and Cryptocurrency:
Blockchain technology and cryptocurrencies address these limitations:

Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other cryptocurrencies have real monetary value
Blockchain provides transparent, verifiable, immutable record of transactions
Decentralized systems eliminate single point of control
Smart contracts enable automated, trustless reward distribution

However, no prior art system has successfully applied cryptocurrency incentives to healthcare adherence with:

Integration with electronic health records (EHRs)
Physician-verified behavior completion
HIPAA-compliant privacy
Meaningful health outcome tracking

Dietary Supplement Quality Crisis:
The dietary supplement industry faces severe quality and authenticity problems:
Industry Statistics:

Market size: $196 billion globally (2024)
U.S. consumption: 77% of adults take supplements
Quality problems: 25-30% of supplements adulterated or contaminated
Mislabeling: 40-60% of herbal supplements contain unlisted ingredients

Quality Issues:

Contamination: Heavy metals (lead, arsenic, cadmium, mercury), pesticides, microbes
Adulteration: Cheaper substitute ingredients, synthetic drugs added to "natural" products
Potency: Actual ingredient content differs from label claims (often 20-80% variance)
Counterfeit Products: Fake supplements sold as genuine brands (estimated 10-15% of market)
Lack of Traceability: No reliable way to track supplement from farm to consumer

Regulatory Limitations:

FDA does not pre-approve dietary supplements (unlike drugs)
Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) compliance is self-reported
Testing is often limited to final product, not raw materials
No mandatory third-party verification
Supply chain opacity enables fraud

Consequences:

Patient safety: Adverse events from contaminated supplements (>23,000 ER visits/year in U.S.)
Healthcare provider liability: Physicians recommend supplements without quality assurance
Insurance issues: Cannot reimburse for supplements without verification
Research validity: Clinical studies using low-quality supplements produce unreliable results

Prior Art in Supply Chain Verification:
Blockchain has been applied to various supply chain tracking applications:

Food safety: Walmart uses IBM Food Trust for produce traceability
Pharmaceuticals: MediLedger for prescription drug tracking
Luxury goods: LVMH uses blockchain to verify authentic handbags
Diamonds: De Beers tracks diamonds from mine to retail

However, no prior art applies blockchain to dietary supplement verification with:

Quality assurance (COA - Certificate of Analysis) integration
Consumer-facing verification (QR code scanning)
Integration with healthcare platforms (EHR, treatment protocols)
Automated drug-herb interaction checking upon supplement verification

The Gap:
There exists no integrated system combining:

Cryptocurrency rewards for verified health behaviors with EHR integration
Blockchain supply chain verification for dietary supplements
Automated quality checking and interaction detection
HIPAA-compliant patient data handling
Physician oversight and verification mechanisms

This invention fills this gap with a comprehensive blockchain-based health incentive and supplement verification platform.

#[pallet::call]
impl<T: Config> Pallet<T> {
    /// Submit health behavior for verification and reward
    #[pallet::weight(10_000)]
    pub fn submit_health_behavior(
        origin: OriginFor<T>,
        behavior: HealthBehaviorSubmission
    ) -> DispatchResult {
        let patient = ensure_signed(origin)?;
        
        // 1. Verify behavior authenticity
        let is_verified = Self::verify_behavior(&behavior)?;
        ensure!(is_verified, Error::<T>::BehaviorNotVerified);
        
        // 2. Check if behavior already claimed (prevent double-claiming)
        ensure!(
            !Self::is_behavior_claimed(&patient, &behavior),
            Error::<T>::BehaviorAlreadyClaimed
        );
        
        // 3. Calculate reward amount
        let reward_amount = Self::calculate_reward(&behavior.behavior_type);
        
        // 4. Check rewards pool has sufficient balance
        let pool_balance = Self::rewards_pool_balance();
        ensure!(
            pool_balance >= reward_amount,
            Error::<T>::InsufficientRewardsPool
        );
        
        // 5. Transfer ABENA coins from rewards pool to patient
        Self::transfer_from_pool(
            patient.clone(),
            reward_amount
        )?;
        
        // 6. Store behavior record on-chain (audit trail)
        let behavior_id = Self::generate_behavior_id();
        let behavior_record = HealthBehavior {
            behavior_type: behavior.behavior_type,
            patient_did: patient.clone(),
            reward_amount,
            verification_source: behavior.verification_source,
            timestamp: Self::current_timestamp(),
            verified: true,
        };
        HealthBehaviors::<T>::insert(&behavior_id, behavior_record);
        
        // 7. Check for achievement unlocks
        Self::check_achievements(patient.clone(), &behavior.behavior_type)?;
        
        // 8. Update patient XP (experience points)
        Self::add_experience_points(patient.clone(), reward_amount / 10)?;
        
        // 9. Emit event
        Self::deposit_event(Event::BehaviorRewarded {
            patient,
            behavior_type: behavior.behavior_type,
            reward_amount,
            behavior_id
        });
        
        Ok(())
    }
}
Verification Logic:
rustimpl<T: Config> Pallet<T> {
    fn verify_behavior(
        behavior: &HealthBehaviorSubmission
    ) -> Result<bool, Error<T>> {
        match &behavior.verification_source {
            VerificationSource::WearableDevice(device_sig) => {
                // Verify device signature (e.g., Apple Watch signed data)
                let device_public_key = Self::get_device_public_key(&device_sig.device_id)?;
                let is_valid = Self::verify_device_signature(
                    &behavior.data,
                    &device_sig.signature,
                    &device_public_key
                );
                Ok(is_valid)
            },
            
            VerificationSource::PhysicianAttestation(physician_sig) => {
                // Verify physician's Dilithium signature
                let physician_public_key = Self::get_physician_public_key(
                    &physician_sig.physician_did
                )?;
                let is_valid = dilithium_verify(
                    &behavior.data,
                    &physician_sig.signature,
                    &physician_public_key
                );
                Ok(is_valid)
            },
            
            VerificationSource::PharmacyData(pharmacy_id) => {
                // Query pharmacy API to verify medication fill
                let pharmacy_confirmed = Self::query_pharmacy_offchain(
                    pharmacy_id,
                    &behavior.patient_did,
                    &behavior.medication
                )?;
                Ok(pharmacy_confirmed)
            },
            
            VerificationSource::LabResults(lab_id) => {
                // Verify lab results show improvement
                let lab_result = Self::get_lab_result(lab_id)?;
                let previous_result = Self::get_previous_lab_result(
                    &behavior.patient_did,
                    &lab_result.test_type
                )?;
                let improved = lab_result.value < previous_result.value;
                Ok(improved)
            },
            
            VerificationSource::SelfReportAudited => {
                // Random audit: 10% of self-reported behaviors audited
                let should_audit = Self::random_audit_selection();
                if should_audit {
                    // Schedule manual review
                    Self::schedule_audit(&behavior)?;
                    Ok(false)  // Pending audit
                } else {
                    // Accept self-report
                    Ok(true)
                }
            }
        }
    }
}

    public_pseudonym: Vec<u8>,  // "Health Warrior #7842"
    score: u128,  // XP or coins earned
    rank: u32,
}

pub enum LeaderboardType {
    GlobalXP,  // All-time XP
    MonthlyXP,  // XP earned this month
    WeeklyXP,  // XP earned this week
    MedicationAdherence,  // % adherence
    ExerciseFrequency,  // Sessions per month
    WeightLoss,  // Pounds lost
    CommunityEngagement,  // Helping other patients
}

impl<T: Config> Pallet<T> {
    pub fn update_leaderboard(
        patient: T::AccountId,
        leaderboard_type: LeaderboardType,
        new_score: u128
    ) -> DispatchResult {
        // Get current leaderboard
        let mut leaderboard = Leaderboard::<T>::get(&leaderboard_type)
            .unwrap_or_default();
        
        // Find patient's entry or create new
        let mut entry = leaderboard.iter_mut()
            .find(|e| e.patient_did == patient)
            .cloned();
        
        if let Some(mut e) = entry {
            // Update score
            e.score = new_score;
            
            // Remove old entry
            leaderboard.retain(|x| x.patient_did != patient);
            
            // Re-insert
            leaderboard.push(e);
        } else {
            // New entry
            let pseudonym = Self::generate_pseudonym(patient.clone());
            leaderboard.push(LeaderboardEntry {
                patient_did: patient,
                public_pseudonym: pseudonym,
                score: new_score,
                rank: 0,  // Will be calculated
            });
        }
        
        // Sort by score (descending)
        leaderboard.sort_by(|a, b| b.score.cmp(&a.score));
        
        // Assign ranks
        for (idx, entry) in leaderboard.iter_mut().enumerate() {
            entry.rank = (idx + 1) as u32;
        }
        
        // Store updated leaderboard
        Leaderboard::<T>::insert(&leaderboard_type, leaderboard);
        
        Ok(())
    }
    
    fn generate_pseudonym(patient: T::AccountId) -> Vec<u8> {
        // Hash patient DID to generate consistent pseudonym
        let hash = blake2_256(&patient.encode());
        let number = u32::from_be_bytes([hash[0], hash[1], hash[2], hash[3]]);
        format!("Health Warrior #{}", number).into_bytes()
    }
}

impl<T: Config> Pallet<T> {
    pub fn update_streak(
        patient: T::AccountId,
        streak_type: StreakType
    ) -> DispatchResult {
        let today = Self::current_date();
        
        let mut streak = Streaks::<T>::get((patient.clone(), streak_type.clone()))
            .unwrap_or(Streak {
                streak_type: streak_type.clone(),
                current_streak: 0,
                longest_streak: 0,
                last_activity_date: 0,
            });
        
        let last_date = Self::timestamp_to_date(streak.last_activity_date);
        
        if today == last_date {
            // Already logged today, no change
            return Ok(());
        } else if today == last_date + 1 {
            // Consecutive day, increment streak
            streak.current_streak += 1;
            streak.last_activity_date = Self::date_to_timestamp(today);
            
            // Update longest if applicable
            if streak.current_streak > streak.longest_streak {
                streak.longest_streak = streak.current_streak;
            }
            
            // Award streak bonuses
            if streak.current_streak % 7 == 0 {
                // 7-day streak milestone
                let bonus = streak.current_streak / 7 * 50;
                Self::award_streak_bonus(patient.clone(), bonus)?;
            }
        } else {
            // Streak broken, reset
            streak.current_streak = 1;
            streak.last_activity_date = Self::date_to_timestamp(today);
        }
        
        // Store updated streak
        Streaks::<T>::insert((patient, streak_type), streak);
        
        Ok(())
    }
}

    pub fn create_batch(
        origin: OriginFor<T>,
        batch_data: BatchCreationData
    ) -> DispatchResult {
        let manufacturer = ensure_signed(origin)?;
        
        // 1. Verify caller is registered manufacturer
        let mfg_record = Manufacturers::<T>::get(&manufacturer)
            .ok_or(Error::<T>::NotRegisteredManufacturer)?;
        
        ensure!(mfg_record.verified_by_abena, Error::<T>::NotVerified);
        
        // 2. Generate unique batch ID
        let batch_id = Self::generate_batch_id(&batch_data);
        
        // 3. Verify batch doesn't already exist
        ensure!(
            !SupplementBatches::<T>::contains_key(&batch_id),
            Error::<T>::BatchAlreadyExists
        );
        
        // 4. Create supply chain event (Manufactured)
        let mfg_event = SupplyChainEvent {
            event_type: EventType::Manufactured,
            timestamp: Self::current_timestamp(),
            location: mfg_record.location.clone(),
            actor_did: manufacturer.clone(),
            signature: batch_data.manufacturer_signature.clone(),
            notes: None,
        };
        
        // 5. Create batch record
        let batch = SupplementBatch {
            batch_id: batch_id.clone(),
            product_name: batch_data.product_name,
            manufacturer_did: manufacturer,
            manufacture_date: batch_data.manufacture_date,
            expiration_date: batch_data.expiration_date,
            batch_size: batch_data.batch_size,
            raw_materials: batch_data.raw_materials,
            quality_tests: vec![],  // Will be added by lab
            supply_chain_events: vec![mfg_event],
            qr_code_url: format!(
                "https://verify.abena.health/supplement/{}",
                batch_id.to_string()
            ).into_bytes(),
            recall_status: RecallStatus::Active,
        };
        
        // 6. Store on blockchain
        SupplementBatches::<T>::insert(&batch_id, batch);
        
        // 7. Emit event
        Self::deposit_event(Event::BatchCreated {
            batch_id,
            manufacturer: manufacturer.clone(),
            product_name: batch_data.product_name
        });
        
        Ok(())
    }
    
    /// Lab uploads test results for batch
    #[pallet::weight(10_000)]
    pub fn upload_test_results(
        origin: OriginFor<T>,
        batch_id: BatchID,
        test_data: QualityTestData
    ) -> DispatchResult {
        let lab = ensure_signed(origin)?;
        
        // 1. Verify caller is registered lab
        let lab_record = Labs::<T>::get(&lab)
            .ok_or(Error::<T>::NotRegisteredLab)?;
        
        // 2. Get batch
        SupplementBatches::<T>::try_mutate(&batch_id, |maybe_batch| -> DispatchResult {
            let batch = maybe_batch.as_mut().ok_or(Error::<T>::BatchNotFound)?;
            
            // 3. Create quality test record
            let quality_test = QualityTest {
                test_type: test_data.test_type,
                lab_name: lab_record.name.clone(),
                test_date: Self::current_timestamp(),
                coa_hash: test_data.coa_hash,
                result: test_data.result.clone(),
            };
            
            // 4. Add to batch
            batch.quality_tests.push(quality_test);
            
            // 5. Create supply chain event
            let test_event = SupplyChainEvent {
                event_type: EventType::LabTested,
                timestamp: Self::current_timestamp(),
                location: lab_record.location.clone(),
                actor_did: lab,
                signature: test_data.lab_signature,
                notes: Some(format!("Test: {:?}", test_data.test_type).into_bytes()),
            };
            
            batch.supply_chain_events.push(test_event);
            
            // 6. Check if any tests failed
            if test_data.result.status == TestStatus::Fail {
                // Initiate recall
                batch.recall_status = RecallStatus::Recalled(
                    format!("Failed {:?} test", test_data.test_type).into_bytes()
                );
                
                Self::deposit_event(Event::BatchRecalled {
                    batch_id: batch_id.clone(),
                    reason: format!("Failed quality test").into_bytes()
                });
            }
            
            Ok(())
        })?;
        
        Self::deposit_event(Event::TestResultsUploaded {
            batch_id,
            test_type: test_data.test_type
        });
        
        Ok(())
    }

rust#[pallet::call]
impl<T: Config> Pallet<T> {
    /// Patient scans supplement QR code for verification
    /// This is called by ABENA mobile app
    #[pallet::weight(5_000)]
    pub fn verify_supplement(
        origin: OriginFor<T>,
        batch_id: BatchID
    ) -> DispatchResult {
        let patient = ensure_signed(origin)?;
        
        // 1. Retrieve batch from blockchain
        let batch = SupplementBatches::<T>::get(&batch_id)
            .ok_or(Error::<T>::BatchNotFound)?;
        
        // 2. Check recall status
        if let RecallStatus::Recalled(reason) = &batch.recall_status {
            // Batch is recalled!
            Self::deposit_event(Event::RecalledProductScanned {
                patient: patient.clone(),
                batch_id: batch_id.clone(),
                reason: reason.clone()
            });
            
            // Award bonus for not purchasing recalled product
            Self::award_coins(patient.clone(), 100 * 10_pow(18))?;  // 100 ABENA
            
            return Err(Error::<T>::ProductRecalled.into());
        }
        
        // 3. Check expiration
        let now = Self::current_timestamp();
        if now > batch.expiration_date {
            return Err(Error::<T>::ProductExpired.into());
        }
        
        // 4. Award coins for scanning (incentive)
        Self::award_coins(patient.clone(), 10 * 10_pow(18))?;  // 10 ABENA
        
        // 5. Check drug-herb interactions (if patient has medications)
        let has_interaction = Self::check_drug_herb_interactions(
            patient.clone(),
            &batch.product_name
        )?;
        
        if has_interaction {
            // WARNING: Interaction detected!
            Self::deposit_event(Event::InteractionDetected {
                patient: patient.clone(),
                supplement: batch.product_name.clone()
            });
            
            // Award bonus for avoiding dangerous interaction
            Self::award_coins(patient.clone(), 100 * 10_pow(18))?;  // 100 ABENA
        }
        
        // 6. Record scan event (analytics)
        Self::record_scan_event(patient.clone(), batch_id.clone())?;
        
        // 7. Return verification result to app (off-chain)
        Self::deposit_event(Event::SupplementVerified {
            patient,
            batch_id,
            manufacturer: batch.manufacturer_did,
            expiration: batch.expiration_date,
            tests_passed: batch.quality_tests.iter().all(|t| t.result.status == TestStatus::Pass),
            interaction_detected: has_interaction
        });
        
        Ok(())
    }
    
    fn check_drug_herb_interactions(
        patient: T::AccountId,
        supplement_name: &Vec<u8>
    ) -> Result<bool, Error<T>> {
        // 1. Get patient's current medications
        let medications = PatientMedications::<T>::get(&patient)
            .unwrap_or_default();
        
        if medications.is_empty() {
            return Ok(false);  // No medications, no interactions
        }
        
        // 2. Query interaction database
        for medication in medications {
            let interaction_exists = InteractionDatabase::<T>::get((
                medication.clone(),
                supplement_name.clone()
            ));
            
            if let Some(interaction) = interaction_exists {
                if interaction.severity == InteractionSeverity::Major {
                    return Ok(true);  // Dangerous interaction found
                }
            }
        }
        
        Ok(false)  // No dangerous interactions
    }
}

### **ABSTRACT**

A comprehensive blockchain-based system for incentivizing healthy behaviors through cryptocurrency rewards and verifying dietary supplement quality through immutable supply chain tracking. The system implements ABENA Coin, a native cryptocurrency token, which patients earn by completing verified health behaviors including medication adherence, exercise, healthy eating, and preventive screenings. Health behaviors are verified through multiple sources (wearable devices, physician attestations, pharmacy data, laboratory results) with cryptographic signature validation. Smart contracts automatically distribute cryptocurrency rewards, track achievements and streaks, and maintain privacy-preserving leaderboards. Patients redeem earned cryptocurrency for health products, health services, insurance discounts, or charitable donations. The system integrates blockchain-based supplement verification enabling manufacturers to record batch information, quality testing results (heavy metals, microbiology, potency, adulterants), and supply chain events immutably. Consumers scan QR codes to verify supplement authenticity, view lab results, and check for drug-herb interactions with automated warnings. The integrated system awards cryptocurrency for scanning supplements and avoiding dangerous interactions, creating synergistic incentives for patient safety and supplement quality assurance. Post-quantum cryptographic signatures (CRYSTALS-Dilithium) ensure long-term security.
